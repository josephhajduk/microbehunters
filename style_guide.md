# Microbe Hunters — Visual Style Guide

## Target Audience
A precocious 5-year-old being read to by a parent.

## Animation Style
Linocut / block print aesthetic. Bold black outlines, flat muted color fills,
rough textured edges, hand-printed look on natural paper.

## Prompt Prefix (prepend to every generation prompt)
```
Simple linocut print style illustration, bold black outlines, flat muted colors in dusty rose and sage green and ochre, rough textured edges, hand-printed look on natural paper, children's book illustration,
```

## Prompt Quality Requirements
- Scenes must be HIGHLY DETAILED and SCIENTIFICALLY ACCURATE
- Describe period-correct clothing, equipment, and setting for each scientist's era and country
- When depicting microscopes, specify the correct type for the era (single-lens vs compound)
- When depicting microbes, use accurate morphology (rod-shaped bacilli, round cocci, flagella, endospores, etc.)
- When depicting lab techniques, use historically accurate methods (staining, culture media, inoculation tools)
- Include specific environmental details: type of lighting, furniture, instruments on the bench
- Prompts should be 60-100 words (including prefix) to capture sufficient detail
- NO words like "warm," "friendly," "beautiful," "magical," "amazing" — these trigger AI slop
- NO artist name references — unreliable in image generators

## Character Consistency Notes
Each scientist should have historically accurate, visually distinctive descriptions held constant across all their scenes:

- **Leeuwenhoek**: round face, big nose, white curled periwig, long dark wool coat, 17th-century Dutch burgher clothing, hunches over tiny hand-held single-lens microscope (brass plates the size of a large coin with glass bead lens)
- **Spallanzani**: tall and thin, sharp features, black Catholic priest's cassock, 18th-century Italian, dramatic gesturing hands, works with sealed glass flasks over flames
- **Pasteur**: neat trimmed beard, round spectacles, formal black frock coat (not white lab coat — he predates that), 19th-century French, intense focused expression, swan-neck glass flasks
- **Koch**: thick dark mustache, round wire spectacles, wool waistcoat with rolled shirtsleeves, sturdy German build, works with compound brass microscope, petri dishes, staining dyes
- **Roux**: gaunt face, dark pointed beard, rolled-up shirtsleeves, French, works with syringes and guinea pigs in a cluttered Paris laboratory
- **Behring**: broad shoulders, Prussian military bearing, blond mustache, formal dress, works with serum and antitoxins
- **Metchnikoff**: wild bushy white beard, round spectacles, Russian, excitable gestures, works with starfish larvae and white blood cells under microscope
- **Theobald Smith**: quiet neat American, clean-shaven, works in a modest USDA laboratory surrounded by cattle tick specimens in glass jars, incubators with blood samples
- **Bruce**: British military uniform with sun helmet, African landscape with tsetse flies, works with blood slides from cattle and patients, field laboratory in Zululand
- **Ross**: British Indian Army officer, thin mustache, works in a hot cramped Indian bungalow with mosquito cages and dissecting needles, sweat-stained shirt, oil lamp
- **Grassi**: dark Italian features, animated expression, points at Anopheles mosquitoes pinned on cards, works in a malaria-ridden Italian village
- **Walter Reed**: US Army uniform, kind weathered face, works in a military camp in Cuba with volunteer soldiers, wooden barracks
- **Paul Ehrlich**: messy cluttered desk covered in chemical bottles, ever-present cigar, coat stained with bright chemical dyes, works obsessively with test tubes of colored compounds and laboratory mice

## Microbe Depictions
- Depict microbes with scientific accuracy appropriate for the organism being discussed
- **Bacteria**: rod-shaped bacilli (anthrax, TB) or round cocci, show cell walls, endospores where relevant, flagella on motile species
- **Protozoa** (malaria parasites, trypanosomes): show accurate morphology — malaria as crescent-shaped gametocytes inside red blood cells, trypanosomes as elongated with undulating membrane and flagellum
- **White blood cells / phagocytes**: large irregular cells engulfing smaller bacteria, visible nucleus
- When showing microscope views, use a circular inset to represent the field of view
- Include accurate staining colors where relevant (Gram stain violet/pink, methylene blue, acid-fast red)
